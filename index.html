There are two main hurdles to getting dental implants...

The #1 Hurdle is often overlooked which is candidacy.


In order for dental implants to last a long time, there has to be enough jawbone to hold the implant. When a tooth is removed and an implant is not placed the jawbone immediately begins to shrink. Far too often, people come to us who’ve already lost too much bone and are no longer candidates. It’s the worst when patients come to us after they’ve put treatment off” for too long because of finances and now cannot get the procedure done.

This brings us to hurdle #2 - Affordability


Regular dental procedures like fillings, root canals, crowns, etc add up over time. The cost procedures list from carecredit.com mentions the average root canal/crown combined would be around $2,500. The human mouth has over 32 teeth. At that price point just 10 teeth would cost $2,500. The cots of root canals, crowns, bridges, etc (patchwork) add up over time.


What’s the difference with that and dental implants


The biggest difference is that dental implants are more expensive UP FRONT and then paid on over time. The blessing with going the dental implant route is that you no longer have to deal with the potential pain or embarrassment of the (patchwork).
